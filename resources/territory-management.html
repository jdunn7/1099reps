Territory Management

Maximize your sales potential through strategic territory planning and optimization

The Importance of Strategic Territory Management

For independent medical sales representatives, effective territory management is the foundation of sustainable success. Your territory is more than just a geographic areaâ€”it's your business ecosystem, containing all the potential clients, relationships, and opportunities that drive your revenue.

Strategic territory management allows you to maximize your productivity, minimize travel time and expenses, and ensure you're focusing your efforts on the highest-potential accounts and opportunities.

Territory Analysis and Planning

Before you can effectively manage your territory, you need to thoroughly understand its composition and potential:

1. Account Segmentation and Prioritization

Not all accounts in your territory have equal potential. Categorize them based on:

  • Current Value: Existing revenue or purchasing volume
  • Potential Value: Estimated future revenue based on size, budget, and needs
  • Strategic Importance: Influence on other accounts, reference potential, etc.
  • Relationship Status: New prospect, developing relationship, established client

Use this segmentation to create tiers (A, B, C) that guide your time allocation and visit frequency.

2. Geographic Mapping and Routing

Minimize travel time and maximize face-to-face selling time:

  • Map all accounts geographically using CRM or mapping software
  • Create efficient routing plans that group accounts by location
  • Schedule visits to minimize backtracking and maximize appointments per day
  • Consider traffic patterns and optimal travel times in your planning

3. Opportunity Analysis

Identify specific opportunities within each account:

  • Current products used and potential for replacement or upgrade
  • Upcoming budget cycles and purchasing decisions
  • Pain points and challenges that your products can address
  • Key decision-makers and influencers for each opportunity

Territory Management Strategies

1. Time Allocation Framework

Develop a framework for how you'll allocate your limited time:

  • A-Level Accounts: 50-60% of your time
  • B-Level Accounts: 30-40% of your time
  • C-Level Accounts: 10-20% of your time
  • Adjust based on specific opportunities and account potential

2. Visit Frequency Planning

Determine the optimal frequency for visiting different account types:

  • A-Level Accounts: Every 2-4 weeks
  • B-Level Accounts: Every 4-8 weeks
  • C-Level Accounts: Every 8-12 weeks or as needed
  • Adjust based on sales cycle, relationship stage, and active opportunities

3. Multi-Channel Engagement

Supplement in-person visits with other communication channels:

  • Email for sending follow-up information and educational content
  • Phone calls for quick check-ins and updates
  • Video meetings for presentations when in-person isn't feasible
  • Social media for sharing industry news and maintaining visibility

Pro Tip

Block specific days for administrative work, planning, and remote communication. Protect your field selling days by scheduling them in advance and minimizing non-selling activities during prime calling hours (typically 9am-4pm).

Territory Performance Metrics

Track these key metrics to evaluate and improve your territory management:

Metric Description Target
Sales per Account Average revenue generated per account Increase by 10-15% annually
Account Penetration Percentage of potential products/services sold to each account 50%+ for A accounts, 30%+ for B accounts
Face-to-Face Selling Time Percentage of work time spent in direct client interaction 60%+ of total work time
Sales Funnel Conversion Percentage of opportunities that convert to sales 25%+ overall, 40%+ for A accounts
Travel Efficiency Ratio of travel time to face-to-face selling time Less than 1:2 (1 hour travel : 2 hours selling)
