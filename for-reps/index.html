Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about becoming an independent medical sales rep

A W2 sales rep is an employee of a company, receiving a regular salary, benefits, and having taxes withheld from their paycheck. A 1099 sales rep is an independent contractor who works for themselves, typically earning commission-only compensation. As a 1099 rep, you're responsible for your own taxes, insurance, and business expenses, but you gain the freedom to represent multiple product lines and set your own schedule.

Earnings vary widely based on your experience, the products you represent, and your territory. Many successful independent reps earn between $100,000 and $300,000+ annually. The key advantage is that you can represent multiple non-competing product lines, potentially multiplying your income compared to a traditional W2 position.

While most companies prefer reps with some sales experience, particularly in medical sales, there are opportunities for newcomers with strong networks and transferable skills. If you're new to the industry, you might consider starting with smaller companies or complementary product lines while you build your reputation and expertise.

Common expenses include health insurance, retirement savings, business insurance, travel costs, marketing materials, CRM software, office supplies, and self-employment taxes. Many of these expenses are tax-deductible, so keep detailed records. We recommend consulting with an accountant who specializes in working with independent contractors.

Non-compete agreements are common in the industry. When representing multiple product lines, it's essential to ensure they don't directly compete with each other. Always review contracts carefully and consider having them reviewed by an attorney. Most companies understand that independent reps will carry multiple lines and structure their agreements accordingly, focusing on direct competitors rather than complementary products.
